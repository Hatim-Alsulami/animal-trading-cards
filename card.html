Grey Wolf

Wolves develop close relationships and strong
socialbonds. They often demonstrate deep
affection for family and may even sacrifice
themselves to protect the family unit.

  • Scientific Name: Canis Lupus
  • Height: 66 - 81 cm (Adult, At Shoulder)
  • Lifespan: 6 - 8 years (In the Wild)
  • Habitat: North America, Greenland, Europe, and Asia

Keen senses, large canine teeth, powerful jaws and the ability to pursue prey at 60 km/h equip the grey wolf for a predatory way of lif. A typical northern male may be about 2 meters long. Standing 81 cm tall at the shoulder. Grey wolves live in packs of up two dozen individuals. A pack consist of an adult breeding pair and thier offspring of various ages.
